Renowned Destinations

Chilika lake, Odisha
  •  400 reviews

Chilika Lake is a brackish water lake and shallow lagoon in the state of Odisha, India. It's the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia and the second largest coastal lagoon in the world. The lake is fed by 52 rivers and rivulets, and its waterspread area ranges from 900 to 1165 square kilometers.

Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh
  •  400 reviews

The name Tawang means "horse chosen". It is said to be the biggest Buddhist monastery in the world outside of Lhasa, Tibet. It is a major holy site for Tibetan Buddhists as it was the birthplace of the sixth Dalai Lama.

Zanskar Valley, Ladakh
  •  400 reviews

Zanskar Valley is eternally famous for its high snow capped mountains and surrounding landscape, featuring geographical attributes that are unique to this region. Reaching this place will introduce you to sparkling clean river waters and a pleasant climate that makes it worth exploring.

Khajiyar lake, Himachal Pradesh
  •  400 reviews

Khajjiar Lake is a small lake in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, India. It's located at an elevation of about 1,951 meters above sea level, between Dalhousie and Chamba Town. The lake is surrounded by a large grassy landscape and evergreen cedar trees.

Spiti Valley, Himachal Pradesh
  •  400 reviews

Popularly known as the 'Little Tibet', Spiti Valley in Himachal Pradesh lies at the heights of the Himalayan Mountains. At a height of 12,500 feet above sea level, Spiti Valley takes pride in being one of the most beautiful places in the country.

Hogenakkal Falls, Tamil Nadu
  •  400 reviews

Hogenakkal is situated at the borders of Karnataka at 46 kms from Dharmapuri. In Hogenakal the river Cauvery enters into Tamil Nadu as a big river with gushing water presentably as a natural falls. The name Hogenakal is derived from Kannada means 'Smoky Rocks'.

Sundarbans National Park, West Bengal
  •  400 reviews

The Sundarbans covers 10,000 km2 of land and water (more than half of it in India, the rest in Bangladesh) in the Ganges delta. It contains the world's largest area of mangrove forests.

Valley of Flowers National Park, Uttarakhand
  •  400 reviews

Nestled high in West Himalaya, India's Valley of Flowers National Park is renowned for its meadows of endemic alpine flowers and outstanding natural beauty.
